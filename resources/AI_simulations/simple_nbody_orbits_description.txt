Simple N-Body Orbit Simulator

This interactive simulation models the gravitational dynamics of 2 to 4 
celestial bodies in real time using classical Newtonian mechanics. 
Users can configure each body's name, mass (in solar, Earth, or Jupiter mass units), 
and initial distance from the primary star (in astronomical units, AU), then 
choose from three numerical integration methods — Euler, Velocity Verlet, 
and RK4 — to evolve the system forward in time. 
The simulation rate is adjustable from a fraction of a day to hundreds of days per simulated second, and the number of substeps per frame can be increased for greater accuracy. Bodies are rendered on an HTML5 canvas with color-coded orbital trails, body labels, and a golden ring highlighting the heaviest body in the system. The canvas supports mouse-wheel zoom, allowing the user to inspect close interactions or zoom out to track long-range orbits, with a one-click reset to the auto-fitted default scale. Built-in presets include the Sun–Earth two-body system and the Sun–Earth–Moon three-body system, and a gravitational constant scaling factor allows exploration of non-physical regimes. The total mechanical energy and z-component of angular momentum are displayed live as conserved-quantity diagnostics.
